BACKGROUND OF MASAKA SOCIAL CENTRE

Masaka social centre was established by the colonial administration in uganda during the 1950s.
The centre was created to provide recreational and social facilities for the local community, as well as to promote social cohesion and community development.

Key individuals involoved in its establishment include:
  • Sir Andrew Cohen, governor of uganda(1952-1957)
  • local chiefs and leaders, suchas kabaka of buganda
    •  The centre has since undergone several renovations and expansions, with contirbutions from:
  • 1.Ugandan government agencies
  • 2.Local and international oraganizations
  • 3.Community members and well-wishers

    • Masaka social centre continues to serve the community, hosting various events, activities, and other programs
